Photography

On this page you will find my work during the Photography topic. Here you will find a description for ISO, F-Stop and Shutter Speed. Below the definitions are examples of high, medium and low ISO, F-stop and shutterspeed.

ISO:

ISO is how bright or dark an image is when taken. The ISO when changed change sthe sesativity of an image and how much light is let in. High ISO's are useually for darker enviromemnts letting more light into the phto. This means lower ISO's are for lighter photos letting minimal light into the shot. Medium ISO is used for images where it is neither too light or dark.

F-Stop:

F-Stop or F-Number is a setting on the camera that can change the focus on a photo as well as the focal view. The F-stop also allows less light into the shot. The higher the f-stop is the less light enters into the camera. This means that the lower the f-stop is the more light enters into the camera

Shutter Speed

The Sutter Speed of a camera is how long the lens is open to take a photo. The shutter speed is usually mesaured in a fraction of a second. Slow shutter speeds allow more light into the camer and are usually used at night. In turn high shutter speeds allow less light into the camera being used when it is sunny or lots of light is present.
